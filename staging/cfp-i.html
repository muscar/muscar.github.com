About

The 2nd International Conference on Web Intelligence, Mining and Semantics (WIMS'12) is organised under the auspices of University of Craiova. The site for the previous edition, WIMS'11, can be found here.

This is the second in a new series of conferences concerned with intelligent approaches to transform the World Wide Web into a global reasoning and semantics-driven computing machine. Then next conference in this series, WIMS'13 will take place in Madrid (Spain).

Call for Industrial Track Papers

Web intelligence, Web mining, Web semantics have been and are of major interest for the research communities worldwide. Many papers and publications in these areas address various issues and open new directions for research and prototyping.

With this view we are calling for software applications and prototypes able to demonstrate the various practical applications of many theoretical research results. The Industrial Track will be looking for early market positioning and validation of new ideas and early stage prototypes and demos. We would like to learn from authors why their idea is better than others and to see a demonstration of an early prototype that can support the novelty and potential of the new idea.

The goals of the Industrial Track are: first giving the opportunity for authors/inventors to present their idea in a friendly environment made of academic peers and some business professionals and secondly, to create a round-table where to discuss the challenges and issues with promoting an academic idea into the industry for prototyping and commercialisation within EU and global context.

Submissions requirements

We are looking for submissions of maximum 3 pages, PDF format, describing the proposed software applications and prototypes. We recommend the following structure as guidelines for the submission: describing the perceived value of the proposed application, why is it better than others already existing, demonstration of the application and results/performance measurements.

The Industrial Track welcomes applications that are in various stages of development ranging from early stage/patented only, in process of piloting and already deployed with a small number of users.
